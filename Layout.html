SELECTED WORK
Recent projects that still make my clients smile
Oasis Audio
Immersive Headphone Launch
Brand Identity • Packaging • Campaign Design
Minimal monochrome visuals, bold typography, and soft gradients for a new audio brand.
I designed the full identity, packaging system, and campaign visuals for launch across
digital and retail.
Oasis Audio approached me at the pre-launch stage with a clear vision: to position
themselves as the refined alternative in a crowded headphone market. The challenge was
to create a brand system that felt premium without being pretentious, and minimal
without being cold. I developed a complete visual language built around high contrast,
precise typography, and gradient-based motion graphics. The identity system included
logo suite, brand guidelines, packaging design for three product lines, e-commerce
assets, and launch campaign materials. We kept the palette strictly monochrome with
strategic uses of soft glow effects to suggest 'afterglow' moments in sound. Every
touchpoint, from the unboxing experience to social templates, was designed to feel
cohesive and intentional.
Dune Devices
Portable Music Player
Product Design • UI/UX • Brand Guidelines
A tactile, almost analog-feeling product identity for a digital-first brand. Clean product
renders, precise iconography, and a flexible layout system for web, print, and packaging.
Dune Devices wanted to bring back the joy of focused listening in an age of distraction.
Their portable music player needed a brand identity that honored analog tactility while
embracing digital precision. I built a system around geometric simplicity, restrained color
(black, white, one accent), and ultra-clear iconography. The identity translated seamlessly
across contexts: sleek product renders for their site, embossed packaging details for
retail, and modular UI components for their app interface. I also developed a
comprehensive brand book and asset library so their internal team could maintain visual
consistency as they scaled. The result feels intentional, timeless, and refreshingly un-
tech-bro.
Opal Skincare
Soft Essentials Line
Brand Identity • Photography • Packaging Design
Subtle, light-driven photography and a quiet typographic system to position Opal as a
calm, trustworthy skincare brand. Deliverables included logo, bottle labels, and e-
commerce visuals
Opal came to me with a clear ask: their skincare line was gentle, effective, and ingredient
-focused, but their visual identity felt generic and overlooked. I redesigned everything
from the ground up, starting with a new logotype that felt softer and more considered.
The photography direction emphasized natural light and minimal styling—no aggressive
shadows or clinical backdrops. I designed a label system that worked across multiple
product formats (bottles, tubes, jars) while keeping a unified look. Typography was kept
quiet and elegant, letting the imagery breathe. We also created a full suite of e-commerce
assets, including lifestyle photography, product detail shots, and social templates. The
rebrand helped Opal feel less like a commodity and more like a trusted ritual.

PROCESS
A simple, thoughtful
way to build your
brand
Every project runs through a clear, structured process so you always
know what's happening next - from idea to final files.
STEP 1
Define the vision
We clarify your goals, audience, and what 'good' looks like for you. This gives us a shared direction before any design work begins.
STEP 2
Map the foundations
I translate the strategy into moodboards, references, and directions. You pick a path, and we refine it together
STEP 3
Design the system
Logo, typography, colour, imagery, layouts — everything is built as a cohesive system so your brand looks consistent
STEP 4
Apply and refine
We test the system on real assets: key pages, packaging, slides, or social content. Feedback is folded in until it feels just right
STEP 5
Deliver and support
You receive organised files, usage guides, and templates. Need help rolling it out? I can stay involved as a design partner

ANWERS
Common questions, clear answers
What services do you provide?
I focus on brand identity, digital design, packaging, and launch visuals. Most projects
combine several of these into one clear, focused engagement.
How does working together usually start?
We begin with a short call to understand your goals, timeline, and fit. If it feels right, I'll follow
up with a proposal and scope.
How long does a project take?
Simple identity refreshes can take 2–3 weeks. Full brand systems with multiple applications
usually run 4–8 weeks, depending on complexity and feedback speed.
Do you offer revisions?
Yes. Each project includes structured feedback rounds so we can refine the work together
without endless back-and-forth.
What types of clients do you work with?
I collaborate with early-stage startups, established brands, and independent creators who
value good design and clear communication.
What is your pricing structure?
Projects are priced per scope, not per hour. After our initial conversation, I'll outline options so
you can choose the level that fits your needs and budget.
Can you work with my existing logo or brand?
Absolutely. We can extend or refine your current identity — tightening the system, updating
applications, and creating guidelines.
